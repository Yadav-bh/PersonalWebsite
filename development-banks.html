Development Banks Analysis

üè¶ Key Points to Analyze Development Banks in Nepal (NEPSE)

This guide covers essential metrics and factors to consider when evaluating development banks listed on NEPSE for investment purposes.

1

Capital Adequacy Ratio (CAR)

What it means: Minimum capital cushion to absorb losses.

Why important: Indicates the financial strength of the bank.

Standard: NRB requires minimum CAR of 11%. Higher is safer.

2

Non-Performing Loan (NPL) Ratio

What it means: % of loans not repaid on time.

Why important: Lower NPL means better loan recovery and asset quality.

Target: Below 2.5% is good for development banks.

3

Earnings Per Share (EPS)

What it means: Profitability per share.

Why important: Shows how much profit a bank is generating for shareholders.

Good EPS: Rs. 20+ is attractive in this sector.

üìå Investor Pro Tips (for Development Banks)
  • Focus on medium-sized banks with clean books and consistent EPS.
  • Prefer those with growing dividend, low NPL, and strong ROE.
  • Track the loan and deposit growth rate quarterly.
  • Avoid those with too many recent mergers or poor governance.
  • Use valuation metrics like P/E, P/B, and Dividend Yield before buying.
