üîç Main Terms of Life Insurance Sector (Nepal)

Term Meaning / Explanation
Premium Income Total income earned from selling life insurance policies. It's the main revenue source.
Net Premium Premium income minus reinsurance cost. Shows actual earnings from policyholders.
Policyholder Fund Total reserve created from premiums to pay future claims. Reflects financial strength.
Actuarial Valuation Mathematical assessment of liabilities and reserves done by actuaries. Ensures solvency.
Life Fund Long-term reserve maintained by insurers to pay out life claims and maturity benefits.
Surrender Value Amount paid to policyholders if they cancel the policy before maturity.
Claim Settlement Ratio (Total Claims Settled / Total Claims Received) ‚Äì Indicates reliability of insurer.
Persistency Ratio Measures percentage of policies renewed after 1st year. Higher = stable business.
Embedded Value (EV) Present value of future profits + net worth. Used to value life insurance companies.
Solvency Margin Shows how much capital is available over regulatory requirement. Indicates stability.
Investment Yield Return on invested premium/reserve fund. Key to profitability.
Sum Assured Guaranteed amount paid on death or maturity under policy.
Bonus (Reversionary) Profit shared with policyholders; added to sum assured. Found in participating policies.
Reinsurance Risk transfer to another company to protect against large claims.
Underwriting Profit Profit from core insurance business, excluding investment gains.
