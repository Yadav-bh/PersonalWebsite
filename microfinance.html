üè¶ Microfinance Sector in NEPSE (Class D Banks)

Microfinance institutions (MFIs) in Nepal are Class D financial institutions, mostly working to provide small loans to rural and low-income groups, especially women. They have high interest income, small capital base, and risky lending portfolios, so they require careful analysis.

üîë Important Points to Analyze Microfinance Stocks in NEPSE

1

Loan Portfolio Growth

What it means: Yearly increase in the amount of loans given.

Why important: Strong loan growth = more interest income.

Warning: Rapid growth without proper monitoring = higher defaults.
2

Non-Performing Loan (NPL) Ratio

Formula: NPL √∑ Total Loan

Good Range: Below 2%

Why important: Low NPL = better loan recovery = safer investment.

3

Why important: Higher spread = higher profit from core business.

Microfinance generally has high interest spreads (15%+).
