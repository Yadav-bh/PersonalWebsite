Finance companies are smaller than commercial and development banks but still operate under NRB. They carry higher risk but potentially higher returns.

1

Capital Adequacy Ratio (CAR)

What it means: Minimum capital required to absorb potential losses.

Why it matters: High CAR means stronger financial cushion.

Benchmark: Must be above 11% (as per NRB).

2

Non-Performing Loan (NPL)

What it means: % of loans not being repaid.

Why important: Higher NPL = riskier finance company.

Healthy NPL: Below 3% is acceptable for this sector.

3

Earnings Per Share (EPS)

What it means: Profit per share.

Why important: Strong EPS means better returns and dividend potential.

Good EPS: Rs. 15+ is considered strong for finance companies.
