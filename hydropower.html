4 Construction Risk & Cost Overrun

Many projects face delays and budget overruns.

That means:

  • More debt
  • Delayed income
  • Late dividends
  • Lower stock performance

5 Loan-to-Equity Ratio (Debt Risk)

Most hydropower projects are highly debt-funded.

If debt is too high:

  • Interest payments reduce profits
  • Weak dividend potential
  • Higher financial risk

6 Dividend Potential (Bonus + Cash)

  • In early years, companies give bonus shares, not cash.
  • After COD and stable income, some start giving cash dividends.
  • Always check: past dividend history + future potential.

7 Transmission Line Risk

If NEA or govt. doesn't build transmission lines in time, electricity can't be sold.

That results in losses, despite generation.

Always check: "Is transmission line ready?"

8 Environmental & Political Risk

  • Delays in Environment Impact Assessment (EIA), forest clearance, etc.
  • Changes in govt. policy, local opposition can delay or cancel projects.
