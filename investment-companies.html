These are companies primarily involved in investing in other NEPSE-listed stocks, real estate, or government securities, aiming to grow net worth and generate returns for shareholders.

ðŸ”‘ Main Points to Analyze Investment Companies

1

Net Asset Value (NAV)

What it means: Total assets minus liabilities per share.

Why important: Tells you if the stock is trading above or below its intrinsic value.

Pro Tip: Buy when Market Price < NAV for value investing.

2

Portfolio Holdings

What to check:

  • Which companies/sectors are they investing in?
  • Are they investing in growth stocks or dividend stocks?

Why important: Their return depends on the performance of invested companies.

3

Dividend Source

Check: Whether dividends are from:

  • Trading gains
  • Dividend income
  • Interest or capital gains

Why important: Consistent source = reliable future dividends.

Look for:

  • Low Market Price compared to NAV
  • Consistent dividend history
  • Good portfolio quality

Avoid: Companies with low transparency or overexposed to volatile stocks. High management cost ratios (eats into your return).
