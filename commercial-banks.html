This guide covers essential metrics and factors to consider when evaluating commercial banks listed on NEPSE for investment purposes.

1

Capital Adequacy Ratio (CAR)

What it means: Minimum capital banks must maintain to absorb losses.

Why important: Higher CAR means the bank is more stable and well-capitalized.

Regulatory Benchmark: NRB requires minimum CAR of 11%.

2

Non-Performing Loans (NPL)

What it means: Percentage of loans not being repaid (bad loans).

Why important: Lower NPL = better asset quality = safer investment.

Good Benchmark: Below 2% is considered strong in Nepal.

3

Net Interest Margin (NIM)

What it means: Difference between interest earned and interest paid, relative to assets.

Why important: Shows profitability of bank's core lending business.

Ideal: 3%+ is generally considered good.
