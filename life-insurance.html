Life Insurance Sector Analysis

🔷 Main Points of the Life Insurance Sector in NEPSE

1 Core Business Model of Life Insurance

Life insurance companies collect premiums from customers and promise a payout on death or after maturity.

They invest the collected funds (mostly in fixed income & government bonds) to generate returns.

2 Types of Policies Sold

  • Endowment Policies – combine insurance + savings
  • Term Life – pure risk cover, low premium
  • Whole Life – lifelong coverage
  • Money Back / Pension Plans – return benefits at intervals

Companies with more long-term policies tend to be more stable and profitable in the long run.

3 Premium Collection Growth

The growth rate of annual premiums (first-year, renewal, and total) indicates future revenue.

Consistent premium growth = strong customer base and increasing trust.

4 Reserve Fund Strength

Life insurers must maintain a life fund or actuarial reserve for future claims.

A strong and growing reserve fund = financial health and trustworthiness.

5 Investment Income

The majority of insurance company profits come from investment of collected funds (not from selling policies alone).

High, stable investment return = stronger profits and dividends.

6 Net Profit & EPS (Earnings Per Share)

EPS shows how much profit each share earns.

Good life insurers maintain consistent or growing EPS.

7 Claim Ratio

Claim ratio = (claims paid / net premium earned)

Lower ratio = company retains more profit.

Very high claim ratio = red flag, unsustainable in long run.

8 Solvency Ratio

Shows the company's ability to meet future obligations.

In Nepal, minimum solvency ratio must be maintained as per Beema Samiti (Insurance Board).

Higher is better (above 150% is considered good).

9 Dividend History

Life insurance companies tend to give bonus shares more than cash.

Regular dividend history indicates financial discipline and profitability.

10 Merger & Capital Growth Impact

Many companies recently increased paid-up capital to meet regulatory requirements (Rs. 5+ arba minimum).

Mergers and right shares have diluted EPS in the short term, but may bring long-term strength.

🟩 Key Financial Indicators to Watch

Indicator Description
Net Premium Income Shows core business growth
Life Fund Long-term strength and reserves
EPS Profitability per share
Net Profit Total earnings after costs
Solvency Ratio Ability to pay future claims
Investment Income Core profit contributor
Dividend Payout Return to shareholders

✅ Investment Tips for Life Insurance Stocks

📌 Look for companies with:

  • High and growing life fund
  • Stable or rising EPS and net profit
  • Solid investment income
  • Reasonable claim ratios
  • Regular dividend history (bonus/cash)

❌ Avoid companies with:

  • Low solvency ratio
  • Weak or declining premium growth
  • High expenses or frequent losses

📈 Long-Term Investment View

Life insurance is a long-term, slow but steady sector.

Good for conservative investors seeking capital appreciation + bonus shares.

EPS and dividend will stabilize after current merger/expansion phase.
